The Alchemist's
Legacy

"Unlike conventional pottery, Jaipur Blue is never made of clay. It is an impossible alchemy of quartz stone powder, powdered glass, Multani Mitti, borax, and water."

The technique traveled from Persia to India in the 14th century, finding its true home under the royal patronage of Sawai Ram Singh II. Each piece is glazed with a mixture of cobalt and copper oxide, creating the signature sapphire hue that never loses its brilliance over centuries.

The firing process is the most precarious stage. At exactly 800 degrees Celsius, the glass powder melts into a translucent skin, locking the hand-painted motifs forever into a vitreous, jewel-like finish that is both brittle and eternal.
